TIME PERIOD

Mara Johnson and Gabi Murphy

INDIGENOUS FEMINISM

Both feminism and ethnic studies have historically had a conspicuous gap where Native women should have fit. This is because many of the aims of both feminist and other minority groups tends to be at odds with Indigenous goals, because they seek complete independence as opposed to equality. Being recognized by the settler state would do little to assuage the very real issues that these communities face and could in fact be detrimental to them. To avoid the risk of potentially despiritualizing and removing the key concept of Indigenous needs from feminism is a big task, but there are many who feel fit to take it on, such as the woman led Sogorea Te Land Trust fighting to regain land from UC Berkeley.
